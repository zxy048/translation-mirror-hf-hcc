═══ Panel E. Cross-Disease Synthesis ═══

Key observations:

1. Module composition differs fundamentally:
   - HF Black module (99 genes): Predominantly non-coding RNAs (snoRNA/scaRNA)
     that guide rRNA chemical modifications, with co-localized ribosomal proteins
     and translation factors. This suggests coordinated regulation at the level of
     ribosome biogenesis and rRNA maturation, rather than the translation machinery itself.

   - HCC Blue module (1315 genes): Larger module with canonical GO enrichment
     for cytoplasmic translation, ribosome structure, and peptide biosynthesis.
     This suggests a broader translational remodeling program in HCC.

2. Hub gene co-localization:
   - HF: 4/7 hub genes in Black module
   - HCC: 4/7 hub genes in Blue module
   - Despite different module compositions, ribosomal protein hub genes
     co-localize in both diseases, supporting the 'mirror regulation' model
     (conserved module structure, disease-specific perturbation direction).

3. Shared translational machinery:
   - Both modules converge on cytoplasmic translation (GO:0002181,
     GO:0006412) despite being identified in completely independent cohorts,
     platforms (microarray vs RNA-seq), and disease contexts.

4. Note on module sizes and detection sensitivity:
   - The HF black module (99 genes) is substantially smaller than the HCC blue
     module due to: (a) the top-3,000-variance filter excluding constitutively expressed
     ribosomal protein genes from WGCNA input, and (b) the fundamental difference in
     transcriptional scope between HF (targeted translational suppression) and HCC
     (broad oncogene-driven translational reprogramming).
